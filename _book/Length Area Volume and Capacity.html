10  Chapter Ten: Length, Area, Volume, and Capacity

Length, Area, Volume, and Capacity

Length, is the distance between two points. Its SI Unit is the meter (m). The perimeter of a rectangle and a square is given by the formula: \[ Perimeter\, of \,a \,rectangle\,=\,2(length+width)\]

\[Perimeter \,of \,a \,square\,=\,4 \times length\]

The circumference of a circle is given by the formula:

\[ \begin{align*} Circumference \,of\, a \,circle &=\pi D\\&=2\pi r \end{align*} \] The length of an arc (l) of a circle subtended by an angle, \(\theta\) , at the center of the circle is given by the formula:

\[ l=\frac{\theta}{360}\times 2\pi r\]

Area, is the amount of surface enclosed within the boundaries of a plane shape. Its SI unit is a square meter (\(m^2\)).

Area of a rectangle (A):

\[A=length (l)\times width(w)=lw\]

Area of square (A):

\[A=length \times length= l^2\]

Area of a triangle (A):

\[A=\frac{1}{2} \times base \times height\]

Area of a parallelogram (A):

\[A=base \times height\] Area of a rhombus (A):

\[A=base \times height \]

OR

\[A=\frac{1st\, diagonal \times 2nd \,diagonal}{2}\]

Area of a trapezium (A):

\[ \begin{align*} A&=\frac{1}{2} \times height\times (sum \,of \,the\, two \,parallel \,sides)\\ &=\frac{1}{2} \times h \times (a+b) \end{align*} \]

Area of a circle (A):

\[A=\pi r^2\]

Area of a sector subtended by an angle  \(\theta\) at the center of a circle:

\[A=\frac{\theta}{360} \times\pi r^2\]

Surface area of solids (S.A)

Surface area of a cuboid:

\[ \begin{align*} S.A&=2(length \times width)+2(width \times height)+2(length\times height)\\ &=2(lw)+2(wh)+2(lh)\\ &=2(lw+wh+lh) \end{align*} \]

Surface area of a cylinder: \[ \begin{align*} S.A&=2\pi rh+2\pi r^2\\&=2\pi r(h+r) \end{align*} \]

Surface area of a sphere:

\[S.A=4\pi r^2\]

Volume, is the amount of space occupied by a matter. Its SI unit is a cubic meter (\(m^3\)).

Volume of a cuboid: \[ \begin{align*} v&=length \times width \times height\\&=lwh \end{align*} \]

Volume of a cube:

\[ \begin{align*} v&=length \times length \times length \\ &=l^3 \end{align*} \]

Volume of a cylinder:

\[ v=\pi r^2 h\] Volume of a sphere:

\[ v=\frac{4}{3} \pi r^3\] Volume of a prism:

\[ \begin{align*} v&=cross-sectional \,area \times length \\ &=\frac{1}{2}\times base\times height\times length\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\times bhl \end{align*} \]

Capacity, is the ability of the container to hold fluids. Its SI unit is the litre (l).\

Conversion of units

\[ \begin{align*} 1 m &=100 cm \\ 1 cm &= 10 mm\\ 1000 cm^3 &= 1 \,litre =1 m^3\\ 1 cm^3& = 1\, ml\\ 10000 m^2 &= 1 \,hectare (ha)\\ 100 m^2 &= 1 \,acre \end{align*} \]

10.1 Solved Examples

Example 1

An angle of 0.9 radians at the Centre of the circle subtends an arc of length 28.8cm. Find in 4 significant figures(\(\pi radians=180^0\)):

  1. The radius of the circle (3mks)

  2. The area of the sector enclosed by the arc and radii. (2mks)

Solution

The angle of the arc \(=0.9 \pi radians\) \ Given that: \(\pi\,rad=180^{0}\),\ The length of the arc (l) is given by:

a)

\[ \begin{align*} l&=\frac{\theta}{360}\times 2\pi r\\ 28.8 cm &=\frac{162^{0}}{\cancel\pi}\times\frac{1}{\cancelto{180}{360}\times \cancel2\cancel\pi r \\ 28.8 cm &= \frac{162^{0}}{180^{0}}r \\ r&= 28.8 cm \times \frac{180^{0}}{162^{0}\\ therefore, r&=32 \end{align*} \] )̱

\[ \begin{align*} Area&=\frac{\theta}{360} \times\pi r^2 \\ &=\frac{162^{0}}{360^{0}\cancel\pi}\times\cancel\pi \times 32 \times 32\\ &=\frac{162^{0}}{360^{0}\times 32 \times 32\\ &=460.8 cm^2 \end{align*} \]

Problems to solve

A solid hemisphere of radius 7cm has the same volume as a cube. Find the length of the cube to 3 significant figures. (3mks)

Mango juice in a factory is stored in a rectangular tank whose internal dimensions are 1.8m by 1.4m by 2.5m one day the tank was 80% full of Mango juice. Calculate the volume of Mango juice in the tank in litres. (3mks)

An open box has an external breadth of 12 cm, a height 10 cm, and a length of 15 cm. If the thickness of the material of the box is 1 cm, find the total surface area of the box. (5mks)

A triangular plot PQR is such that the length of the side PQ is two-thirds that of QR. The ratio of the lengths \(PQ: PR = 4:9\) and the angle at Q is obtuse. If the perimeter of the plot is 38m calculate the length of the 3 sides of \(\triangle PQR\) (4mks)

A metallic cuboid 10cm by 12cm by 15cm is melted. Half of it is used to make a cylinder of radius 4.2cm, the remaining is used to make a sphere. Determine in 4 significant figures using \(\pi= \frac{22}{7}\) \begin{enumerate}

The height and surface area of the cylinder to 2 decimal places. (4mks)

The radius and surface area of the sphere are correct to 3 significant figures. (4mks)

The difference between the surface area of the sphere and the cylinder. (2mks)
